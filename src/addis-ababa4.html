Overview of the Addis Ababa Corridor Project

The Addis Ababa corridor project is an urban initiative focused on improving mobility and infrastructure. Currently, several corridors are being developed, featuring dedicated bicycle lanes and green spaces. These enhancements aim to reduce traffic congestion and promote eco-friendly transportation, ultimately elevating the quality of life for residents.

Corridor Developments

The Addis Ababa City Corridor project offers a range of corridor types designed to enhance urban mobility, accessibility, and aesthetic appeal. Each corridor incorporates features like strategic lighting, green spaces, and visually appealing designs that add beauty to the cityscape while ensuring safe, efficient, and sustainable passageways for all residents.

Bicycle Lanes

Dedicated lanes for cyclists improve safety and accessibility while promoting a sustainable mode of transportation, encouraging more residents to ride and enjoy the outdoors..

Green Spaces

Integrated parks and green zones add aesthetic appeal and recreational areas, improving air quality and offering relaxing spots for residents.

Road Enhancements

Each corridor includes expanded and repaved roads to ensure smoother traffic flow, safer intersections, and better vehicle access throughout the city

Telecommunication Poles

Added infrastructure for telecom poles improves connectivity, supporting both local residents and businesses by ensuring reliable communication networks.

Pedestrian Pathways

Safe, well-maintained sidewalks and crossings promote pedestrian accessibility and safety, making walking a more viable option in these corridors.

Recreation Areas

Recreation areas are designed for leisure and outdoor activities, providing communities with spaces for relaxation, sports, and social gatherings. These environments promote mental well-being and foster a sense of community.

Bridges

Strategically constructed bridges facilitate transportation and trade, enhancing connectivity and economic growth in Ethiopia. These structures improve access to remote areas, promote regional development, and ensure safer travel routes.

Waste Management Systems

A water management system ensures efficient distribution and conservation of resources, supporting community needs and promoting sustainability.

Riverside Project

The Ethiopian Riverside Project, commonly known as the "Beautifying Sheger" initiative, is a comprehensive urban development endeavor launched in 2019 under Prime Minister Abiy Ahmed. Its primary objective is to revitalize Addis Ababa by transforming the city's riversides into vibrant public spaces, thereby enhancing residents' quality of life and boosting tourism.

SEGMENT-01

Segment-1 Entoto Natural and Cultural Zone

Starting at the Entoto Mountains, renowned for their panoramic views and rich cultural heritage, this segment highlights ancient churches such as St. Mary of Entoto, a historic pilgrimage site. It also features the Entoto Natural Park, offering hiking trails, picnic spots, and eco-tourism activities. Surrounded by eucalyptus forests and fresh mountain air, this area provides a serene and refreshing environment. The purpose of this segment is to preserve and showcase the natural and historical significance of Entoto while serving as a gateway to the rest of the corridor.

Segment 2: Scenic Transition Zone

This segment connects the serene Entoto highlands to the urban periphery, creating a seamless transition between natural landscapes and the city. Landscaped roads with tree-lined avenues and scenic viewpoints dominate this area, offering a tranquil journey. Dedicated cycling and pedestrian paths emphasize sustainability and eco-friendly mobility, while minimal commercial development ensures the preservation of the areaâ€™s natural aesthetics.

Segment 3: Historic and Cultural District

As the corridor moves into Addis Ababaâ€™s historic zones, this segment focuses on cultural landmarks and historic sites. Visitors can explore museums, monuments, and traditional markets along the route. Road improvements and better signage make these cultural treasures more accessible, while spaces for exhibitions and handicrafts celebrate the cityâ€™s heritage.

Segment 4: Urban Commercial and Residential Hub

This midpoint segment transitions into densely populated urban areas, showcasing a mix of residential buildings, offices, and retail spaces. Public transport options such as buses and taxis are upgraded, and streetscape enhancements like modern lighting, green spaces, and benches improve the areaâ€™s livability. This segment blends urban functionality with a vibrant community atmosphere.

Segment 5: Entertainment and Social Spaces

Moving closer to the Peacock area, this segment emphasizes recreation and social interaction. It features cinemas, theaters, and family-friendly parks, as well as restaurants and nightlife spots. These spaces are designed for gatherings, events, and entertainment, making the route an engaging destination for locals and visitors alike.

Segment 6: Peacock Urban Center

The final segment culminates in the bustling Peacock Urban Center, a vibrant hub of commerce and modernity. High-density commercial zones with shopping malls, corporate offices, and hotels dominate this area. Smart traffic management systems ensure smooth commuter flow, while urban green spaces provide pockets of relaxation amidst the busy cityscape. This segment establishes Peacock as a dynamic endpoint connecting the historic Entoto area to Addis Ababaâ€™s modern core.
